Efficiency Tips for Engineers
Efficiency Tips for Engineers Talk Notes
2024-07-20 10:36 folder Misc   label Productivity   Habits  

Ref: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b33vqX74EcA (Talk given by Mo Tiwari)

Things to know in 20s

  • Determine what’s important
  • On your deathbed, what do you want to say you have done?
  • Write down your goals, and stick them on your desk
    • Spend time with friends and family
    • Exercise
    • Finish your work project
  • Everything else, cut as much as possible

Profile your time

  • You would run a profiler to see where code spends its time
  • Profile yourself!
  • Self-logging post hoc is notorious
  • RescueTime App
    • Highest productivity at 80%
    • Good weeks are around 65%
  • Is your time spent aligned with your goals?
  • Organize and build systems
  • How much is your time worth

Startegy

1. Reuse, don’t redo

  • Taro example of building a web app
  • Engineering
    • Use existing frameworks as much as possible
    • Open Source is great!
  • Research/Math
    • Literature review
    • Often don’t need the detailed proofs
  • Strategic Tip(find your nearest neighbor)
    • Reuse as much as possible

2. Make decisions in advance

  • Decision-making on-the-fly tends to be very poor
  • Influenced by emotions and biases
  • Run the scenarios in advance and decide what you’ll do in each
    • ex. If X happens, I’ll do Y

3. Important vs. Urgent

          ^
          | Schedule | Do Now  |
Important |---------------------
          | Don't do | Delegate|
          |--------------------->
                  Urgent

4. Engineering Vs. Research

  • Engineering:
    • Problem and solution are known
    • Largest costs are execution time
    • Prioritize (highest-value / time) first
    • Prioritize getting end-to-end system working
  • Research:
    • Problem and solutions are unknown
    • Most things will fail; that’s expected
    • Your responsibility is to fail fast and reveal that if possible
    • Monkdy on a pedestal
      • Decision between train monkey to recite Shakespeare or build a pedestal
      • Train monkey to recite Shakespeare (Hard to success but bring more value in long term)
      • Build a pedestal (Easy to achieve in short term and bring little value)

5. Motivation

  • Motivation succeeds action, not precede
  • Just get started!
    • Stress is highest when you’re thinking about starting
    • Waiting doesn’t make the water warmer
  • “Do it for 5 minutes”
  • Leave a “breadcurmb” task for tomorrow morning
    • “Make your bed” principle
  • Swallow the toad (Do the hardest thing first!)
    • Stress integrated over time will be lower

6. Procrastination !!!

  • NOT about laziness or willpower
  • Rather, it’s about emotional management
    • Perceived effort and perceived reward
    • Perceived effort is often very different from actual effort
  • Different people have different emotional responses to different tasks
  • Get severe enough to lead to avoidance
    • Scroll phone, play video games, etc.
    • Then punish themselves and feel even worse about the task
  • Lower activation energy
    • Reward yourself after completes the task
    • Do it with friends/colleagues

Operations

  • Build in time for breaks
  • Dopamine fasts (Doing absolutely nothing)
    • After 15 minutes, you’ll be so bored you want to do your work
  • Meditation
  • Time-blocking
    • with buffers
  • Energy audit
    • Can I condition myself to be MORE energized?
  • Pomodoro technique
    • Work for a fixed amount of time, rest for a fixed amount of time

Focus and phones

  • The importance of focus
  • Deep work
  • Phones:
    • Lead to anxiety and hypervigilance
    • Reading books, talking to family, more peace
    • Greyscale it!
    • Challenge: go without your phone for one day

Tactics

Efficiency: tech

  • “I’m doing this a lot. How can I do it faster?”
  • Get to know your hotkeys!
    • Terminal
    • IDE
    • Control you Mac
    • Challenge: go without your mouse for a day
  • Get more screens
  • Systems
    • 1-click meetings
    • Document your frequent communications

Efficiency: behaviors

  • “No imaginary rules”
  • Speed-reading
    • Swiftread
  • Speech-to-text
  • Memorize common function names / theorems
    • Don’t look up every time
    • Should be able to write programs from scratch / without references
  • Print out papers / reading!
  • If something takes < 2 minutes, do it now

Cheatsheet

  • Say “no” to as much as you can
    • Decline meeting invites
  • Be clear about what can be satisficed vs. optimized
    • Timebox these things
    • Paradox: choosing between two close choices is harder than two disparate ones
  • Have a routine
    • Schedule your day in advance
  • Read!
